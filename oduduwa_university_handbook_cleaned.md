## HISTORICAL BACKGROUND

Oduduwa University, named after the Yoruba ancestor (Oduduwa), is a private, state-of-the-art higher institution having academics, elites, professionals and business moguls drawn from the public and private sectors as members of the Board of Trustees and the Governing Council respectively.

The University, a modern tertiary institution, is conceived not only as an ultimate response to Government's call towards contributing to educational development efforts in the country by the private sector, but also to serve as a catalyst in the educational development of the people within its catchment area in particular and the world in general.

The University seeks an exploration of new insights in objectives and values with a readiness to deal with the ambiguities produced by rapid changes and sensitivity to social, psychological and global problems based on keen awareness to scientific developments.

Oduduwa University was established by Prof. Ramon Adegoke Atobatele Adedoyin in 2006. It finally got approval of The Federal Government/National Universities Commission on 3rd November, 2009.

Prof. Adedoyin had been in the business of education for about 40 years, doing almost the same thing every day. He is a frontliner in the business of Education in Nigeria.

## INTRODUCTION (Programme Overview)

The Programme aims at training manpower that will be relevant in the business sectors of the economy through the use of modern innovations in Information Technology for financial reporting and other allied services.

**Programme Duration:** Minimum of four (4) years for students admitted by UME, and five (5) years for courses in Environmental, Health Sciences and Engineering.

**Admission Requirements:** At least five (5) O'Level credit passes in SSCE, NECO or equivalent in not more than two (2) sittings, plus acceptable UTME cut-off marks (which may vary per year).

**Graduation Requirements (minimum units):**
- UTME & Post-UME admission candidates: 173 units
- Direct Entry admission candidates: 131 units

**Examinations:** One week is normally provided for revision before examinations start. A university-wide timetable is provided; examinations are scheduled for the last three (3) weeks of the semester. Failure to sit an examination without satisfactory reason results in a grade of F for the affected course.

**Absence from Examination:** Must be supported with a medical certificate reported to the Head of Department on time; forwarded to the College Board for approval.

**Examination Malpractice:** Penalty is EXPULSION, which may lead to refusal of admission to other Nigerian universities. Forging certificates, transcripts, or other admission documents leads to expulsion.

### Scoring and Grading (NUC-approved standard)

|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Vary, assigned per course/week/semester and workload | 70–100 | A | 5 | 4.50–5.00 | 1st Class |
| | 60–69 | B | 4 | 3.50–4.49 | 2nd Class Upper |

| | 40–44 | E | 1 | | |
| | 0–39 | F | 0 | | |

CGPA is derived by summing quality points (Grade Point × Credit Unit) across all courses examined, divided by total credit units.

**Worked Example (Year One):**
- 1st Semester: Total Quality Points (TQP) = 45, Total Credit Units (TCU) = 16 → GPA = 45/16 = 2.81
- 2nd Semester: TQP = 44, TCU = 15 → GPA = 44/15 = 2.93
- Cumulative (Year One): TQP (both semesters) = 89, TCU (both semesters) = 31 → CGPA = 89/31 ≈ 2.87

### Change of Programme
Students may not normally change programme until after completing the first academic year; some departments impose further restrictions even after year one. Applications go through the Head of Department and Provost, in the prescribed form (quadruplicate), obtainable from the Registry (Academic Division).

### Intra-College and Inter-College Transfers
Acceptable, but transfer to professional programmes (College of Natural and Applied Sciences, College of Management and Social Sciences, College of Environmental Design and Management, College of Health Sciences, College of Law) requires a minimum CGPA of 3.5 at the time of application.

### Inter-University Transfer
Students from another university may apply, enclosing relevant credentials and transcripts to the Registrar. Applicants must be of good standing, with CGPA not less than 3.0.

### Expulsion/Suspension
A student expelled or suspended from another university for misconduct is not eligible for transfer to Oduduwa University; a minimum residency of two years elsewhere is required for any inter-university transfer consideration.

### Duration of Programmes
Minimum 4 years, maximum 6 academic years. A CGPA below 1.50 at the end of a session requires withdrawal from the department (with possible transfer to another department). After 6 years without a degree, a student is asked to withdraw from the University.

### Academic Adviser
Advisers are appointed per level per department to provide academic and other guidance. Students are encouraged to consult their level adviser for academic problems.

### Leave of Absence
Students may apply for one year (renewable up to a maximum of 2 years), in advance, through the Head of Department and Provost, for Senate consideration.

### Matriculation
All registered students take part in the matriculation ceremony as stipulated in the University Calendar. Matriculation numbers are issued after the ceremony and used throughout the student's studies to identify assignments, term papers, answer booklets, etc.

### Course Registration
- First two weeks of the academic year are for course registration (week 1 for late registration at additional fee, week 2 for normal registration).
- Registrations are carried out at the student's parent department, who ensure prescribed requirements are met per course.
- Students are encouraged to join their respective professional associations.

### Examination Malpractice Offences (non-exhaustive list)
1. Plagiarism
2. Copying laboratory/fieldwork reports and term papers of others
3. Colluding with staff to obtain or provide exam questions/answers beforehand
4. Colluding with staff to submit a substitute answer script after an exam
5. Writing a project/lab/field report on behalf of a student by a staff member
6. Soliciting help after an examination
7. Breaking into a staff/departmental office to obtain exam materials or substitute scripts
8. Refusal to cooperate with College Investigation Panel
9. Manipulating registration forms to sit for unqualified exams
10. Sitting in for an exam one is not registered/qualified for
11. Colluding with a medical doctor to obtain a feigned-illness excuse
12. Producing a fake medical certificate
13. Assault/intimidation of invigilators
14. Destroying/attempting to destroy evidence of malpractice
15. Staff intimidating/threatening students to extort sex, money, or other favours in exchange for grades

**Punishment:** Rustication or outright dismissal after College-level investigation, with ultimate decision by Senate. Staff found guilty face disciplinary action.
